Emotional Intelligence and IQ.


I really enjoyed the research part iunder sprint 3-core. The research on the above topic could not have happened in a better timing. I revisited facts I knew but I also deepened my prior understanding as well as discovered new facts which I will be sharing with you in this blog today.
Lets’ start from the beginning, shall we?


1. What is Emotional Intelligence?

Many definititions can be found online, and they all point out the same things. That Emotional intelligence (EQ) is the ability to identify, use, understand, and manage emotions in positive ways in order to relieve stress, communicate effectively, empathise with others, overcome challenges, and defuse conflict.
In other words emotional intelligence can definitely impact the many different aspects of our daily lives, starting from the way we behave and the way we interact with others.


2. How is EQ different to IQ?

Intelligence quotient or IQ, is a number derived from a standardized intelligence test. On the original IQ tests, scores are calculated by dividing the individual's mental age by his or her chronological age and then multiplying that number by 100.
IQ represents abilities such as:


  • Visual and spatial processing

  • Knowledge of the world

  • Fluid reasoning

  • Working memory and short-term memory

  • Quantitative reasoning

Whereas Emotional Intelligence EQ, on the other hand, is a measure of a person's level of emotional intelligence. IT refers to a person's ability to perceive, control, evaluate, and express emotions.

EQ is centered on abilities such as:

  • Identifying emotions

    •
  • Evaluating how others feel

  • Controlling one's own emotions

  • how others feel

  • Using emotions to facilitate social communication

  • Relating to others

  • One's level of empathy

3. How does Emotional Intelligence looks like?

A person with a high emotional intelligence has the ability to identify and recognise their own emotional state as well as the emotional states of other beings around them. They also engage with others in a way where others feel drawn to go towards them. They use their understanding of emotions in order to relate better to other people, and in doing so, they have the ability to create healthy relationships with beings in their daily life. It also impacts on their ability in achieving a greater success at work. In brief, a person with a high EQ or EI leads, being always as their optimal level, lives a more fulfilling life.In my research on the subject, I was surprised to learn that Emotional intelligence consists of four key attributes, which I will now be sharing with you.


1. Self-awareness

Which guides us in being able to identify and recognise our very own emotions. I can also help us in going one step deeper where with self-awareness, we are able to notice as well how these emotions we feel can affect our thoughts and of course, in a domino effect, our behaviour. It also helps us in noticing and accepting our strengths and limitations, without having any change to our level of self-confidence.


2. Self-management

It brings me back to my experience in Early childhood, where we would plan strategies to help and support tamariki in regulating their emotions and responses to them. Self-management is in other words emotional regulation where we are able to control these impulsive feelings and behaviours that do pop out from time to time, and sometimes when we expect it the least. This attribute of emotional intellingence helps us in managing our emotions in a healther way. It also enoucourages us in being proactive and in taking initiative, following through with our numerous commitments, and lastly, it is seen in the ability to adapt to change.


3. Social awareness

With this awareness, we are able to understand the emotions, needs, and concerns of people around us in our daily routine. We are also able to pick up on emotional cues. We feel comfortable socially, and we can easily recognise and identify the different dynamics existing within a team, a group of people or an organisation.


4. Relationship management

We are able to create and maintain genuine relationships, where we showcase clear communication, we can even inspire and influence others around us. We often can be seen as a team-player, and we show great skills in regards to conflict-management.


4. In which instances do I need to manage my Emotional Intelligence?

In life of course, many things are unpredictable and many a times,when we encounter certain situations, we do react rather than pause reconnect to our growth mindset and respond. Some of these tricky situations where the ability to “self-manage” our emotions kind of goes pass us and through the window are:

  • when we are giving and receiving feedback.

    •
  • when we need to meet a tight deadline.

  • when we are dealing with a challenging relationship.

  • when we do not have enough resources.

  • when we have to deal with change.

  • when we have a setback or do not succeed at something.

Among many others.


5. How can I get better and have a better level of Emotional Intelligence?

Fortunately, a lot of studies have shown that EQ can be learned and developed. Her are three simple strategies one can use, to develop the right habits in this regard:

1. Attention training

Through this training, we develop the ability of paying attention to what is happening in our body, which then leads us to understand our emotions, how we feel like this and why. As this technique is practiced,we can train our attention in paying a close awareness to our mind, our body and of course our emotions. The attention training nurtures us in developing the skills needed to reach a clear, calm and alert mind.


2. Self knowledge and Mastery

Developing an attentive mind, we are then able to see what is really going on on an internal level. Going back to this level, we are able to develop a clearer knowledge of who we are, how we are and how we engage and respond to different types of situations. And from developing this knowledg, we move towards mastery. Our awareness of how we feel and respond in specific situations also brings along the possibility of auto-regulation.


3. Creating Useful Mental Habits

Re-wiring our brains with positive stories and thoughts for ourselves and others. Instead of saying “I did not succeed, I am a total failure”, train your thoughts in saying “You are on the right path, continue your efforts, you will eventually are doing great”
